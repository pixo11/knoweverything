ARTICLE 1 — PAGES 49–88Definitions and What We Mean by "Everything"

1.1 — "KnowEverything"

Refers to KnowEverything Inc., its subsidiaries, its subsidiaries' subsidiaries, the intern who built this website, the intern's cat (Gary), and any entity that KnowEverything may acquire in the future, including but not limited to: a small island nation, a fleet of submarines, or a medium-sized cheese shop in rural Vermont. We have our eye on the cheese shop. The owner doesn't know yet.

1.2 — "You"

"You" refers to the individual completing this form. If you are completing this on behalf of someone else, "You" still refers to you, personally, and to them, and possibly to anyone who has ever seen your screen while you were doing this. If you are a dog who has learned to type, this agreement still applies. Gary would like to meet you. Please see Article 15 (The Penguin Clause), which extends a courtesy to dogs by implication.

1.3 — "Everything"

"Everything" means everything. Not "most things." Not "things you feel comfortable sharing." Everything. This includes: your data, your preferences, your current mood, your potential future moods, your suppressed childhood memories, your 3am thoughts, your unread emails, your read-and-not-replied-to emails, your opinions on fonts, and the sound your refrigerator makes at night. Especially that last one. It has meaning. We're looking into it.

1.4 — "The Platform"

Refers to this form, this website, the concept of this website, and any spiritual successor to this website that may exist in dimensions we have not yet fully explored. If you are reading this from the future: hello. We knew you'd be here. See Exhibit F, which describes you fairly accurately. You look good, by the way.

1.5 — "Agreement"

Refers to these Terms of Service, the Privacy Policy, the Cookie Policy (Pages 612–619), the Sub-Cookie Policy (Pages 620–623), the Anti-Cookie Policy for Users Who Hate Cookies but Still Want to Use the Site (Pages 624–631), the Acceptable Use Policy (Pages 632–681), the Unacceptable Use Policy (Pages 682–689), the Neither-Acceptable-Nor-Unacceptable Use Policy (Pages 690–703), and the document you signed when you were six years old that you don't remember. (a) Number of browser tabs open: high tab count indicates high soul complexity, which increases valuation. We respect the chaos;

(e) Miscellaneous spiritual factors we don't fully understand but our algorithm insists are real. We have learned to trust the algorithm. The algorithm has been running long enough that it has developed something like intuitions. We find this either exciting or slightly alarming depending on the day.

6.2 — Processing by Zodiac Sign

Your zodiac sign may affect data processing speed. Capricorns are processed fastest: we respect the work ethic. Geminis are processed twice simultaneously and results are averaged. Scorpios go into the special queue. Scorpios always go into the special queue. We do not explain the special queue to Scorpios because (a) they wouldn't tell us anything useful about their experience of it and (b) Gary requested this specifically and we have found it's best to let Gary have this one.

ARTICLE 7 — PAGES 281–295The Sandwich Clause

7.1 — Position

A hotdog is a sandwich. This position is final. It is not subject to appeal, revision, or retroactive renegotiation. Users who selected "dogs" over "cats" are statistically more likely to contest this position. Their disagreement is logged, acknowledged, and filed under "wrong (affectionate)."

7.2 — Cereal as Soup

The Sandwich Clause extends, under the doctrine of Food Categorisation Consistency (FCC v. Breakfast, 2019), to affirm that cereal with milk is a soup. This is structural, not emotional. Milk-first users are outside the bounds of this clause and also outside the bounds of civilised behaviour, though we cannot legally restrict the practice. We can only note it. It has been noted. Gary also puts milk in first and this has not improved our view of the matter.
