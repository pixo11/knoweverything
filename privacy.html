A BRIEF SUMMARY FOR PEOPLE WHO SKIP TO THE END: We collect your data. We use it. We store it. We think about it. Gary has access to the database. We are still dealing with that. The rest of this document explains all of the above in more words.

SECTION 1What We Know About You

1.1 — What You Told Us

You filled out 100 questions. We have all of it. Your name, address, zodiac sign, dominant hand, relationship status, diet, sleep schedule, opinion on toilet paper, spirit animal, post-apocalypse survival strategy, genie wish, tombstone text, and the number of browser tabs you had open. All of it is in a spreadsheet. The spreadsheet is colour coded. We are still not telling you the colour system.

1.2 — What We Inferred

Our algorithm has generated the following from your answers: your approximate stress level at the time of completion (detectable from answer speed on open text questions); whether you paused before the zodiac question (most people who selected "I don't believe in this" paused — we have thoughts); whether your spirit animal matches your self-image or your aspirational self (these diverge more than expected); your 11pm refrigerator frequency; whether you answered the "how dramatic are you" question dramatically (a meta-result we treasure).

1.3 — What Gary Knows

Gary has access to the same database as the rest of the team. Gary cannot read. Gary can, however, sit on the keyboard at statistically impactful moments. On March 14th, 2023, Gary's intervention resulted in the accidental deletion of 47 user records and the creation of one record belonging to no one, which had answered every question with "7." That record still exists. We cannot delete it. We have grown attached to it. Its spirit animal is listed as "a Roomba." We think that's fitting.

SECTION 3Who Else Sees Your Data

3.1 — Internal Access

The full team (4 humans, 1 bot named Gerald who only sends thumbs-ups, and Gary). Gary should not have access. We have changed the password twice. Gary was present both times and appears to have memorised it. We are exploring solutions including a keyboard tray with a lock, a decoy spreadsheet, and simply accepting that Gary is part of this organisation now in a way that exceeds his original mandate as "office cat."

3.2 — Third Parties

Service providers: Companies helping us run the platform. They see your data for technical reasons. They are not supposed to have opinions about it. Some probably do. Analytics partners: They receive anonymised data. Except in cases where "most users" did something unusual, in which case it's fairly clear who it was. We are looking at the person who selected both "avocado (overpriced)" and "I work for exposure." You know who you are. Research institutions: One researcher described the raccoon subgroup as "the most unexpectedly coherent group I've encountered in 15 years of work." We feel seen. Advertisers: We're telling you directly. The Privacy Policy is where we keep our honesty.

3.3 — Who We Will Not Share With

We will not share with: your parents (unless you ask); your ex (considered and rejected); anyone who would use your tombstone text against you; or the government, except as required by law, which we include here knowing it sounds sinister but is genuinely just a legal formality. Probably.

SECTION 4Cookies

Essential cookies: Required for the form to function. Analytics cookies: 73% of users pause on the toilet paper question longer than any other. 12% change their answer. 3% change it back. We find this deeply human. Gary cookies: Appear when Gary walks across the keyboard during deployment. Their behaviour is unpredictable. They affect the colour scheme in ways we cannot reproduce. We have left them in because removing them caused worse problems.

SECTION 5Your Rights

5.1 — Right to Access

You may request a copy of your data. Several people have and reported it was "weirder than expected" and "more accurate than was comfortable." We consider both outcomes successful.

5.2 — Right to Correction

You may request correction of factual inaccuracies. You may not contest computed results (personality word, Soul Value™, energy profile). You may submit a written objection. The objection will be weighed against the algorithm. The algorithm has a compelling track record.

5.3 — Right to Deletion

We will delete your data. The process involves six systems, one of which is a spreadsheet Gary may have locally cached somewhere. We will make a sincere and documented effort. The record of our effort to delete your data is itself stored as data. We understand the irony.

5.4 — Right to Not Receive Your 10-Year Message

You may opt out. We ask you to reconsider, because the people who most want to opt out are usually the ones who most need to receive it. The opt-out form is in the Tuesday newsletter, under "Things We'd Rather You Didn't Do But We Respect Your Autonomy."

SECTION 6The Gary Situation

Gary is a cat who, through keyboard interventions and what security professionals describe as "statistically improbable coincidences," has acquired access to portions of our database exceeding his intended role as ambient morale support. Three internal reviews have concluded Gary has not misused his access, largely because Gary is a cat and does not have goals in the traditional sense. We acknowledge this is not a satisfying security finding. We are monitoring the situation.

The honest bit:

Most Privacy Policies exist to protect the company. This one does too. But it also exists to tell you the truth: you filled out a form about yourself, and now someone has that information, and that's a kind of small trust, and we don't take it lightly.

Your data is yours. We're just holding it. Like a very strange museum.

Puedes solicitar corrección de inexactitudes factuales. No puedes impugnar resultados calculados (palabra de personalidad, Valor del Alma™, perfil de energía). Puedes presentar una objeción por escrito. La objeción se sopesará contra el algoritmo. El algoritmo tiene un historial convincente.

Haremos un esfuerzo sincero y documentado. El registro de nuestro esfuerzo para eliminar tus datos está en sí mismo almacenado como datos. Entendemos la ironía.

Puedes optar por no recibirlo. Te pedimos que lo reconsideres, porque las personas que más quieren no recibirlo suelen ser las que más necesitan hacerlo. El formulario de exclusión está en la newsletter del martes, bajo "Cosas Que Preferiríamos Que No Hicieras Pero Respetamos Tu Autonomía."
